# Textbook Content Use Policy

Textbook is a personal, educational, noncommercial template for turning Markdown-heavy repositories into private study apps. This policy is not legal advice; it is a practical rule set for using the template responsibly.

## Allowed Uses

- Use Textbook for personal study, hobby projects, research, classroom, charity, or other noncommercial work.
- Ingest content that you created, content you have permission to adapt, or content with a license that allows the copying, transformation, and deployment you plan to do.
- Keep generated course apps private by default, especially when the source license is unclear.
- Preserve upstream attribution, links, commit references, and license notices.

## Do Not Use Textbook To

- Sell access to generated courses or use generated courses in a commercial product or paid service.
- Copy, transform, or republish private, paid, proprietary, or paywalled content without explicit permission.
- Remove or hide source attribution, license notices, author names, or required notices.
- Publish a generated course when the upstream license is missing, unclear, or does not allow redistribution or derivative works.

## Source Repository Checklist

Before ingesting a repository, check:

1. Does the source repository include a license file?
2. Does that license allow copying and adapting the content, not just viewing it on GitHub?
3. Are there third-party assets, diagrams, images, datasets, or excerpts with separate licenses?
4. Are attribution, notice, and share-alike requirements preserved in `ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS.md` and the generated app?
5. If you deploy the generated app, is it private unless the source license clearly allows public redistribution?

If the answer is unclear, do not publish the transformed content. Ask the rights holder for permission or use your own material instead.

## Important Boundary

The Textbook license covers the Textbook template code only. It does not grant rights to any third-party content you ingest. Source content remains governed by its original license and any other applicable rights.
